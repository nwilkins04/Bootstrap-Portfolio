About Me

My name is Nicole Wilkins. I currently hold a B.A. and M.A. from UCF in teaching.
Growing up with a cousin on the Autism Spectrum Disorder scale, I knew that working with others like her is exactly what I wanted to do. So off to college I went! I studied hard after four years I graduated with my Bachelors. Then got into graduate school and earned a grant to pay for it. So off to teaching and grad school I went! I worked hard during the day with very difficult students, and studied very hard at night. After two years I graduated with my Masters. (First on in my family I might add).
For the past 6 years I have been an ESE teacher, doing one of the most challenging jobs physically and mentally that I could think of. What got me through the aggressive nature of my students was seeing the long-term progress. Knowing that what I was teaching them were life skills that they would always have. While I always saw the progress that not only I was making, but that my students were making, my bosses did not. I was always told the things that I was not doing correctly, the things that my students should be doing , but were not.
After having one of the worst years I could imagine, I decided that I desperately needed a career change. This however, is not as easy as you may think. What am I supposed to do? I've gone to school for so many years to do this one thing, I excell in this one thing, I volunteer in this one thing. Stuck? Confused? You bet! After lots of talking with family, friends, and research. finally figured it out! Technology! Thats where everything is headed. Thats what my students love, technology! So here I am ready to learn a new skill to help those that see and explore the world differently. Hopefully my diverse background will give me skills that I can bring to the table different then others!
